Research

Research Areas

Explore the diverse research areas within our department, including:

Harmonic Analysis & Functional Inequalities

Research in this area focuses on extending classical inequalities (Hardy, Sobolev, Stein-Weiss) to non-Euclidean geometries governed by Dunkl operators, differential-difference tools for symmetric spaces. Key contributions include fractional calculus adaptations, trace theorems, and variational methods for PDEs in singular or reflection-invariant domains. This work bridges harmonic analysis, geometric measure theory, and mathematical physics, with collaborations advancing extremal functions and weighted Sobolev spaces.

Operator Algebras & Quantum Structures

This work explores planar algebras, subfactor theory, and their connections to quantum information and mathematical physics. Highlights include combinatorial constructions (e.g., Latin squares), Fourier-theoretic inequalities for C*-algebra inclusions, and equilibrium states (KMS conditions) in quantum statistical mechanics. Collaborations reveal deep ties between algebraic structures, entanglement, and quantum computing, offering tools for both foundational theory and applied quantum systems.

Spectral Synthesis & Approximation Theory

Pioneering studies on Wiener-Ditkin sets and spectral synthesis address stability under unions/intersections and approximation limits in harmonic analysis. Foundational results include nonsynthesizable sets, weak Ditkin properties, and spectral behavior on locally compact groups. Collaborative efforts refine ideals in function algebras, with implications for Fourier analysis and topological group theory.

Programmes

Doctoral Programme (Ph.D.)

The Department of Mathematical Sciences at Kannur University offers a Doctoral Programme (Ph.D.) for students interested in pursuing advanced research in various areas of mathematics.

The program provides a stimulating environment for in-depth study and original research under the guidance of experienced faculty members. Research areas may include pure mathematics, applied mathematics, and interdisciplinary fields.

The program involves rigorous coursework and significant independent research culminating in a dissertation.

M.Sc. Mathematics Programme

The M.Sc. Mathematics program at the Department of Mathematical Sciences, Kannur University, is a comprehensive two-year postgraduate course spread across four semesters.

It is designed to provide students with an advanced understanding of various branches of pure and applied mathematics. The curriculum focuses on developing advanced knowledge in areas such as algebraic structures, real and complex analysis, topology, functional analysis, differential equations, and more. The program aims to enhance mathematical reasoning, problem-solving skills, and the ability to construct rigorous mathematical proofs.

Eligibility for admission typically requires a B.Sc. Degree in Mathematics with a minimum of 50% marks. Admission is generally based on the marks secured in the qualifying degree examination.
